Big Cities Make a Lot of Pollution—We Can Help Them Change

What’s happening?

Big cities, such as New York and Beijing, produce a significant amount of pollution due to the heavy use of fossil fuels for transportation, electricity, and industry. This pollution not only dirties the air but also worsens climate change.

How can we solve that as a group?

Cities can reduce their carbon footprints by switching to green energy sources and improving public transportation. Governments can incentivize companies to use clean energy, and cities can create policies that encourage sustainable urban planning.

How can I solve that as an individual?

If you live in a city, you can help by using public transportation or carpooling to reduce the number of vehicles on the road. Supporting clean energy initiatives in your community and conserving electricity at home can also make a significant difference.
