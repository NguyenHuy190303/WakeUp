The Air is Getting Dirtier, But We Can Clean It

What’s happening?

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) levels are increasing in the atmosphere due to human activities such as transportation, industrial processes, and burning fossil fuels. This buildup is driving climate change, making the planet hotter and leading to more frequent natural disasters like wildfires.

How can we solve that as a group?

We can collectively reduce CO₂ emissions by transitioning to clean energy sources like wind and solar, switching to electric vehicles, and planting more trees to absorb CO₂. Governments and communities should promote policies that encourage green energy adoption.

How can I solve that as an individual?

You can contribute by driving less, using energy-efficient appliances, and planting trees in your communities or homes. Reducing personal energy consumption by turning off lights and unplugging devices also helps to lower CO₂ emissions.
